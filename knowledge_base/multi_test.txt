**Article 1: The Propulsion Breakthrough**
The Ares-X mission is set to use the new 'Vortex Ion Drive' developed by Ion Dynamics. This engine allows for 50% faster travel to Mars. Chief Engineer Leo Grant claims the drive is stable.

**Article 2: The Life Support Challenge**
The Future Habitat Group has completed the 'Bio-Dome 2' life support system for the Ares-X mission. Dr. Aris K. led the project, which uses genetically modified moss to produce oxygen. Funding was provided by the Global Space Agency.

**Article 3: Mission Control and Funding**
The Global Space Agency (GSA) announced today that the Ares-X mission has reached its funding goal of $10 billion. The mission will be overseen by Director Sarah Vance. GSA is coordinating with Ion Dynamics and Future Habitat Group to synchronize the launch in 2027.
